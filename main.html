The Shortcut Coding Clubs

The Shortcut Coding Clubs are a space where anyone who is interested in coding can come and learn regardless of their background and experience. The clubs consiste of peer-to-peer learning where participants help each other and learn from one another. You can check out each club's monthly topics in the calendar.

Python Club

Python is one of the most popular and high demand programming languages and its versatility is what makes it invaluable for beginners. Learning a dynamic coding language like Python is a great foundation for growth in your career

Why Python?

Python is one of the most popular and high demand programming languages and its versatility is what makes it invaluable for beginners. Learning a dynamic coding language like Python is a great foundation for growth in your career.

Who is this for?

No previous experience with Python is required and people of all levels are welcome to enable a positive peer to peer learning experience.

What will you learn?

The programming concept, what it means to code.

Programming practices (how to make coding productive, programming in a team, version control, code reviews etc.)

A complete understanding of Python from the ground up.

How?

The club runs once per week with two hour sessions using peer to peer learning.

When and where?

Every Monday starting February 18 from 17.00 to 19.00 at The Shortcut Lab.

Please NOTE! You have to bring your own laptop! Register through: https://www.eventbrite.com/e/python-club-tickets-56803335305

JavaScript Club

The JavaScript Club is a peer learning club where you have the chance to learn through hands-on sessions that will get you acquainted with JavaScript

Why JavaScript

JavaScript is the most popular programming language in the world, working on a variety of platforms and devices. The job market requires more and more JavaScript coders so learning it is the starting point for new career opportunities. The Shortcut aims at creating a community of coders who are interested in technology and eager to share their passion with others.

What

The JavaScript Club is a peer to peer learning club where participants have the chance to learn through hands-on sessions that will get them acquainted with JavaScript.

How

Each session is comprised of discussions and peer learning where you can learn from experienced JavaScript coders. Those who already have some knowledge on JavaScript can support/share/ teach others. We’ll also provide various workshops on Git, Azure, AWS. Their exact dates will be announced later in the event page and by email.

For whom

The JavaScript Club is open to everyone eager to learn and advance step by step each week. If you don’t have previous knowledge you are welcome. If you are already acquainted with JavaScript and you would be willing to share your knowledge, you are also welcome.

When and where

Every Tuesday from 16:30 to 18:30 at The Shortcut Lab. No registration required, but we would appreciate if you let us know you’re coming by dropping a message at don@theshortcut.org

React Club

The ReactJS Club is an advanced peer-learning group of coders who wish to take their coding skills to the next level, learn a new JavaScript library and put their newly acquired skills into practice through exciting projects

Why ReactJS

Coding is the language you have to speak in 2019. The ReactJS Club continues this year too and is open to everyone interested in learning by doing.

What

ReactJS Coding Club is a peer to peer learning club where participants will have hands-on coding sessions to get acquainted with ReactJS.

How

Each session will take you through different coding and learning sessions where you will learn from your peers, discuss and solve puzzles to build up projects. All this will result in projects that can be used to showcase your newly acquired skills.

For whom

The ReactJS Club is open to everyone, regardless of the background and experience. If you don’t have previous knowledge you are welcome. If you are already acquainted with React and you would be willing to share your knowledge, you are also welcome.

When and where

Every Thursday from 16:30 to 18:30 starting January 17th at The Shortcut Lab. No registration required, but we would appreciate if you let us know you’re coming by dropping a message at don@theshortcut.org. Come as you are (with your own laptop)

Girls' Coding Club

Learning how to code used to be hard, lonely and mostly done by boys. Not anymore!

The Girls' Coding Club club meets on a weekly basis and its purpose is to give the opportunity to women to improve their coding knowledge . The club is open to anyone who supports women in tech. This is not a class. It is a club where participants get together and learn without teachers (except for special occasions). We use FreeCodeCamp curriculum to grow to knowledge and skills little by little. The aim is to get started with learning to code and then support each other to keep going. Each week we are working on different topics going step by step through HTML, HTML5, CSS, JavaScript. A Hackathon every 6 months is organised by the club participants to test how their skills have evolved and validate the learnings.
